## üìù Section 2: Advanced Prompt Frameworks
Moving beyond "simple asks." Use these frameworks to get expert-level output.

### 1. The RIDE Framework (Clinical Education)
* **Role:** You are a senior attending in [Specialty].
* **Input:** Based on the following patient history and lab results: [Paste Data].
* **Directive:** Generate a differential diagnosis ranked by probability.
* **Examples:** For each diagnosis, provide one supporting lab value and one contradictory finding.

### 2. The Chain-of-Thought (CoT) Trigger
*Add this to any complex request:* > **"Let‚Äôs think step-by-step. First, identify the pertinent positives in the labs; second, cross-reference with the patient's comorbidities; third, propose a management plan based on 2025 guidelines."**

### 3. The "Adversarial Auditor" Prompt (Red-Teaming)
*Use this to verify your own work:* > **"Act as a Chief Medical Officer reviewing the clinical plan below. Identify three potential safety risks, drug-drug interactions, or renal dosing errors. Be highly critical and cite specific contradictions."**

- [ ] **The "Human" Test:** Does this output align with my clinical intuition? If it feels "off," it probably is.

## üó∫Ô∏è Section 4: Workflow Orchestration Map
**Goal:** Chain tools together to eliminate errors.

1. **Discovery:** Use **Scite.ai** or **Consensus** to find the top 5 peer-reviewed papers on a clinical question.
2. **Grounding:** Upload those 5 papers to **NotebookLM** to extract specific protocols and contradictions.
3. **Drafting:** Use an **Institutional GPT** to draft a patient letter or resident briefing based *only* on the NotebookLM notes.
4. **Final Audit:** Run the final draft back through a **Reasoning Model** with an "Adversarial" prompt.
